The number: a package of loose-leaf tea sits at $20 — but the spread is wide

Across 1,990 retail tea packages, the median price is $20.00. The middle half sells between $12 and $30, and the market stretches from a $9 floor to a $38 ceiling at the 90th percentile. Tea has an even wider proportional spread than specialty coffee — pack size and leaf grade vary enormously.

Almost nothing is discounted — just 1.8%

Of 2,990 live variants, only 54 (1.8%) were marked down — less than a third the discount rate we found in coffee (5.8%). Loose-leaf tea almost never goes on sale; it competes on origin, freshness, and story. So when a competitor does discount, it's a real signal worth catching the day it happens.

Methodology. Every figure was pulled live from each seller's public product catalog (the same data their storefront serves) in July 2026 — no estimates, no surveys. "Package" = variants priced $6–$45, the standard retail tin/pouch range. Medians are used to avoid distortion from samplers and bulk SKUs. The full dataset was collected in about a minute with a small script.
